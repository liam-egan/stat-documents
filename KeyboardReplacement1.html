  1. Dell Chromebook A35

Keyboard Replacement

Thanks to the way the A35 is designed and constructed, the keyboard and touchpad assembly is the body of the device. Take apart a HP G4 or G5 and you will see the opposite applies - the keyboard and touchpad are separate compenents that simply lift out of the body.

Before repairing any device, verify that the student whose Chromebook you are about to repair does not have insurance.


Materials:

  • Screwdriver
  • Pry tool
  • New keyboard
  • Magnetic screw stray

Time: 15-20 minutes


  1. Turn off the device, if you have already not done so.
  2. Turn the device over
  3. Remove the 7 Phillips-head screws on the bottom of the device
    1. Always keep your screws organized!
  4. Using a flathead screwdriver or pry tool to pry the bottom panel of the device off.
  5. Try to stay away from the I/O ports, as the metal cutouts are fragile and prone to breaking if subjected to too much force.  Pry near the bottom of the left and right sides or the bottom of the device, near the touchpad and palmrest.
    2. Image by Dell.
    3. It is best to pry off the backplate in the areas highlighted red. Ignore the part outlined in yellow, which is Dell's fault since I stole that image from their website.

Refer to the internal map of the Dell A35 for the next steps. The numbers in parentheses (#) map to the labels on the internal map.

Image by the author.

  1. Everything inside needs to come out.
    1. Unplug the display connector at the top of the motherboard (6).
    2. Remove the two silver and two black screws holding down the battery (7). Unplug it and set it aside.
    3. Unplug and remove the speakers (8 & 9).
    4. Unplug the touchpad (15) and keyboard ribbon cables (14) from the motherboard.
    5. Remove the three silver screws holding down the daughterboard (10).
    6. Unplug the WiFi antennas from the WiFi card and remove the cables from their channels on the fan (2 & 3).
    7. Remove the single black screw holding the fan down (2).
    8. Unscrew, but do not remove, the four black screws holding the heatsink (1) down.
    9. Remove the two silver screws holding the motherboard (4) down.
    10. Detach the small board under the battery (16) from the body of the computer.
    11. You can now remove the motherboard, daughterboard, fan, heatsink, and charging LED board as a single unit and install it into the new keyboard using the screws you removed before.
      1. The ribbon cable connecting (11) the mother and daughterboards is held down by adhesive, which will require some force to remove.
    12. Plug the motherboard and touchpad ribbon cables on the new keyboard into the motherboard.
    13. Install and plug in the speakers, battery, and charging LED board.
    14. Unscrew the three black screws holding down the display hinges (12 & 13) on the old keyboard.
    15. Make sure nothing is connected between the display and keyboard. You should have already disconnected the WiFi antennas and display connector, but double check.
    16. Act as if you are going to open the device while it is still lying upside down. Open the keyboard so it is 90 degrees (or approx. 1.6 radians), then close it again.
    17. Pull the old keyboard away and set it aside. Take the now-separated display assembly and place it display-side up on the table. Grab the new keyboard, with the motherboard and other components inside and screwed in, and align it keyboard-side down. The two pieces should fit snugly together.
    18. Push the hinges down onto the back of the keyboard. They should align and click into place.
    19. Screw the display hinges into the new keyboard.
    20. Reattach the WiFi antennas to the WiFi card, making sure to route the WiFi antenna cables through the guides on the fan. A tool with a small, pointy end here is very helpful here. Use the color guides on the WiFi card to figure out which wire goes where. Use your fingers to align one cable at a time and your small tool to click the little wires into place.
    21. Replace the backplate.
